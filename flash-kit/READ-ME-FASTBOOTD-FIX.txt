FASTBOOTD FIX - why STEP-2 failed and what to do
=================================================

The first STEP-2 tried to flash from the BOOTLOADER fastboot
(the 60s-timer screen). On your Unisoc chip that mode reports
"boot partition size: 0" and cannot write boot -> it failed
safely (nothing was written, your phone stayed stock).

The fix: flash from FASTBOOTD instead - the userspace mode you
rooted init_boot in. These two new scripts do that.

HOW TO USE
----------
1. Put BOTH new .bat files in the SAME folder as boot.img and
   the platform-tools folder (your existing kernel-kit folder).
2. Phone ON, unlocked, USB cable in, USB debugging ON.
3. Double-click  STEP-2-FASTBOOTD-FLASH.bat  and follow it.
   - It reboots into fastbootd, confirms "is-userspace: yes",
     then flashes boot and reboots.
4. If anything looks wrong, close the window and send Viktor
   the text. Do not reboot on a FAILED flash.

IF THE PHONE WON'T BOOT afterwards (recovery):
   - Hold POWER ~10s to force off.
   - Hold VOLUME DOWN + plug in USB  -> bootloader fastboot.
   - Double-click  UNDO-FASTBOOTD.bat  -> restores stock kernel.
   (fastbootd lives in init_boot/vendor_boot, which we never
    touch, so it is always reachable even if boot is bad.)

Nothing here touches init_boot, vendor_boot, thermal, or your
data/banking. Root (Magisk) is preserved.
